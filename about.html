About

Fahrenheit 451 was written by Ray Bradbury on October 19, 1953. It is one of the best works Ray Bradbury has done. It presents an American society where books were prohibited from all. The “firemen” burn all books if any are found. The novel follows Guy Montag, a fireman who burned books. Bradbury was inspired by the book burnings in Nazi Germany. The book won the American Academy of Arts Letters Award in Literature and the Commonwealth Club of California Gold Medal. Ray Badbury was born in 1920 in Waukegan, illinois. He moved with his family to Los Angeles, california. Bradbury has published more than 30 books and has written nearly 600 short stories; he became one of the best writers of scientific fiction. And will always be remembered for his unique style of writing.
